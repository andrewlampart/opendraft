### MINOR (Nice to Fix)

**Issue 2: Terminology Precision ("Eco-management")**
- **Location:** Section 2.3 (Analysis & Results), last paragraph
- **Problem:** The text references "ekozarządzanie {cite_040}" (eco-management) in the context of implementing EMS changes. In Polish, *ekozarządzanie* usually refers specifically to environmental management (ISO 14001), which seems disconnected from Emergency Medical Services unless used metaphorically for "system ecosystem."
- **Fix:** If referring to the "ecosystem of healthcare," use "zarządzanie ekosystemem usług" or simply "zarządzanie zmianą" (change management). If the citation actually refers to environmental management, clarify the relevance.

**Issue 3: Methodological Emphasis Balance**
- **Location:** Section 2.2 vs. Section 2.3
- **Problem:** Section 2.2 places heavy emphasis on the "Narrative Review" (qualitative), but Section 2.3 (Results) is dominated by the "Economic Simulation" (quantitative). The qualitative results (analysis of acts/documents) are mentioned but overshadowed.
- **Fix:** In Section 2.3, add a brief subsection or paragraph explicitly summarizing the qualitative findings (legal barriers identified) before diving into the simulation numbers, to mirror the methodology structure.

**Issue 4: Acronym Definition**
- **Location:** Section 2.3
- **Problem:** "WHO" is used in point 4 without prior definition in the text provided (though likely defined in full text).
- **Fix:** Ensure "Światowa Organizacja Zdrowia (WHO)" is defined at first use (likely in Intro or Lit Review).
